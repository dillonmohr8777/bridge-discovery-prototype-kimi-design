# Bridge North Star synthesis

Prepared from Tori's July 19, 2026 handoff and the supplied source files.

## Brand direction

- Public name: Bridge
- Core promise: Connect. Grow. Fight Together.
- Signature accent: Deep Purple `#4B0082`
- Core neutrals: black `#000000`, white `#FFFFFF`, dark gray `#1A1A1A`
- Headlines: Poppins, bold and confident
- Subheads and controls: Montserrat Semibold
- Body: Inter Regular
- Visual language: high contrast, clean, minimal, purposeful, industry focused, connected, strong
- Imagery: authentic cannabis culture, products, people, and bridge architecture. Avoid generic green-leaf branding.
- Voice: confident, professional, real, purposeful, respectful, empowering

## Product principles

- Trust over engagement
- Connection over popularity
- Discovery over distraction
- Verified information over viral information
- Businesses before influencers
- Community over competition
- No public likes, follower totals, or public vote history
- Community survey responses remain private and appear only in qualified aggregates
- Private contact details move only through permissioned introductions

## Required audiences

- Adults who participate in community activity and daily surveys
- Brands
- Retailers and dispensaries
- Verified cannabis-industry professionals
- Business administrators and reviewers

## Verification and compliance

- Verify legal entity, EIN evidence, cannabis authorization, applicant authority, and business contact ownership.
- Never display EINs, identity records, or uploaded verification documents publicly.
- Support pending, more information required, verified, suspended, and denied states.
- Keep a review trail for submissions, sources, decisions, renewals, and administrative changes.
- Make age, location, license, advertising, medical versus adult-use, delivery, warning, and geofencing rules configurable by jurisdiction.
- Treat a verified badge as confirmation of identity and licensing checks, not an endorsement.
- Keep the platform useful without direct cannabis transactions.

## Cross-route design contract

- The landing route is the public front door. Its primary entry action opens Community without separating the other product spaces into a different prototype.
- Landing and Signal Exchange use the dark brand expression.
- Community, Campaign Studio, and Business OS use a calm light workspace expression.
- All routes share the same purple, typography, radius, button, badge, navigation, focus, and safe-area tokens.
- Desktop app routes use the same left navigation and top bar.
- Mobile app routes use the same four-destination bottom navigation.
- The landing route uses the same four-destination mobile dock so the full product is visible immediately.
- Each interior route ends with a connected handoff to the next product space and a return to the platform overview.
- Mobile layouts collapse to one primary column below 680px.
- Touch targets remain at least 44px, inputs render at 16px on mobile, and bottom navigation respects device safe areas.
- Marketing copy stays concise. Operational detail appears inside the relevant product route.

## Current prototype lanes

1. Community: daily private signals, discovery, useful feed, follows, saves, reposts, and permissioned messaging.
2. Campaign Studio: objective-led creative with audience, market, format, and review context.
3. Business OS: public and B2B profiles, verification, roles, directory discovery, and aggregate insights.
4. Signal Exchange: privacy-conscious signals, explainable opportunity matching, and permissioned introductions.

## Geographic discovery prototype interpretation

- Tori's source prototype uses connected state nodes, state-by-state coverage, and location-specific business discovery.
- The legal strategy requires location-aware and jurisdiction-aware experiences, including configurable state rules and geofencing.
- The source material does not prescribe Google Maps, a 3D city view, or real-time regional signal percentages.
- The live 3D view is therefore an optional interface interpretation for regional discovery, not a source requirement and not evidence of market movement.
- City selectors provide jurisdiction context and an external Google Maps business search. Google listings must never be represented as Bridge-verified records.
- The prototype must not display precise market percentages, sample sizes, or time-window claims on the map until they are backed by a named qualified data source.
- The static regional render remains a resilient fallback for visitors without a Maps JavaScript API session.
- Any live 3D layer must use a domain-restricted Google Maps JavaScript API key, retain privacy copy, and keep Bridge verification visibly separate from Google results.
